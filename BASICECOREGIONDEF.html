Pineywoods

The East Texas region is mostly composed of a thick forest of pine trees. This large forest is part of a much larger "region of trees" that extends into many neighboring states! The terrain is hilly with low, wet bottomlands that grow a variety hardwood trees such as elm, mesquite and ash. This region is home to a variety of plants and animals whose habitat preferences are woodlands and shorelines. Among them are: cottonmouth snakes, squirrels, rabbits and opossums. Swamps are also particularly common, and they are (mostly) located in the southern most area of the region which is known as the “Big Thicket.”

Gulf Praries and Marshes

The Gulf Coastal Plains and Marshes Ecoregion is Texas's main coastal ecoregion! It is vaguely defined as being inside a coastal band from the state boundary of Lousiana to the city of Brownsville 61 miles wide.
